SECTION 1: THE POST-MATRIC VISION Hoye Secondary School does not just aim for a Matric certificate; we aim for a career. This document serves as a roadmap for learners to move from the classrooms of Bergville to the lecture halls of South Africa�s top Universities and TVET Colleges.
SECTION 2: UNDERSTANDING THE CAO (CENTRAL APPLICATIONS OFFICE) For all Universities and Colleges in KwaZulu-Natal (UKZN, DUT, MUT, UNIZULU), learners do not apply to the schools directly.

What is CAO?: It is the central hub for all KZN tertiary applications.
The "On-Time" Deadline: Applications usually close on 30 September for most degrees. Medicine and some Health Sciences close as early as 30 June.
Application Fee: A non-refundable fee (approx. R250) is required. Mrs. Xaba and Miss Hlubi provide guidance on how to pay this at Checkers or via EFT.
CAO Number: Once you apply, you receive a CAO number. You must keep this number safe!
SECTION 3: NSFAS & FINANCIAL FUNDING (THE "NO-MONEY" SOLUTION) Lack of money should never stop a Hoye learner from studying.
What is NSFAS?: The National Student Financial Aid Scheme. It covers Tuition, Accommodation, Books, and a monthly Food Allowance.
Requirements: Your combined household income must be under R350,000 per year.
Documents Needed: Certified ID copies of the learner and parents, and proof of income (SASSA letter or payslips).
When to Apply: Applications usually open in September/October of the Matric year. Miss Hlubi hosts a "Computer Lab Week" to help Matrics apply online.
SECTION 4: UNIVERSITY VS. TVET COLLEGES
Universities (UKZN, WITS, UP): Focus on academic and theoretical degrees (Teaching, Law, Engineering, Medicine). Requires a Bachelor's Pass.
Universities of Technology (DUT, MUT): Focus on practical, career-oriented diplomas (Nursing, IT, Maritime, Accounting). Requires a Diploma Pass.
TVET Colleges (Mnambithi TVET): Focus on artisan skills (Electrician, Plumbing, Engineering Studies, Farming). Requires a Higher Certificate or Grade 9/10/11 completion.
SECTION 5: THE APS SCORE "REALITY CHECK"
As discussed in the Academic file, your APS score is your "currency."
35+ Points: Competitive for Engineering and Pharmacy.
28+ Points: Good for B.Ed (Teaching) or BA (Law).
20-25 Points: Entry for many Diploma courses at DUT or MUT.
Note: If your APS is below 20, the AI will suggest looking into Higher Certificate courses to bridge the gap.

SECTION 7: THE "FUNZA LUSHAKA" BURSARY For learners who want to become teachers and come back to serve schools like Hoye:
Coverage: Full cost of study.
Commitment: You must agree to teach at a public school for the same number of years you were funded.
Priority Subjects: Math, Science, and African Languages (IsiZulu).
SECTION 8: THE "GAP YEAR" WARNING Hoye Secondary strongly discourages "Gap Years" unless for serious medical reasons. Statistics show that learners who take a year off often never return to study. We push every learner to have at least one "Plan B" (a TVET college) if "Plan A" (University) doesn't work out.
SECTION 9: HOW TO CHOOSE A CAREER (MISS HLUBI�S TIPS)
Don't follow friends: Just because your best friend is doing Nursing doesn't mean you should.
Check the Market: Is there a job for this in Bergville or KZN?

SECTION 10: TERTIARY FAQ (THE AI "FUTURE" BRAIN)
Q: Can I apply to University with my Grade 11 results? A: Yes! Most universities give "Provisional Acceptance" based on your final Grade 11 marks.
Q: What is a "Red Flag" on my application? A: Having a "Pending" or "Incomplete" status because you forgot to upload your ID or your parents' proof of income.
Q: Does NSFAS pay for private colleges? A: Mostly no. NSFAS is for public Universities and TVET Colleges. Be careful of "Fly-by-night" private colleges that are not registered.
Q: What is the NBT (National Benchmark Test)? A: Some universities (like Wits or UCT) require you to write an extra test in Math and English. Check your email daily!

SECTION 11: THE MNAMBITHI TVET & LOCAL COLLEGE PATHWAY Not everyone needs a University degree to be successful. For many Hoye learners, the Mnambithi TVET College (Ladysmith/Estcourt campuses) is the best choice for fast employment.

Engineering Studies (N1-N6): Be an Electrician, Mechanical Engineer, or Civil Engineer.
Business Studies: Human Resources, Marketing, and Public Management.
Requirements: Usually requires a Grade 9, 10, or 12 certificate. It is much cheaper than University and is fully funded by NSFAS.
SECTION 12: THE SAPS, SANDF, AND EMS PATHWAY For learners who want to serve the country in uniform (Police, Army, or Paramedics):
SAPS (Police): You must have a Matric certificate with no criminal record. You must be physically fit and between 18 and 30 years old.
SANDF (Army): Applications usually open in January/February. They look for healthy learners with good Math and Science marks for specialized units.
Nursing/Paramedics: You must apply through the KZN Department of Health. Life Sciences (Biology) is a mandatory subject for this path.
SECTION 13: THE "PLAN B" - WHAT IF I FAIL MATRIC? Failure is not the end of the road at Hoye. The AI should guide learners to these options:
The Second Chance Matric Programme: Register with the Department of Education to rewrite only the subjects you failed.
Remarking: If you are 1% or 2% away from a pass, you can pay a small fee to have your paper remarked.
Supplementary Exams: Usually written in May/June of the following year.
SECTION 14: DIGITAL LITERACY & THE CAO PORTAL Since SKAVS is an AI, we must emphasize digital readiness.
The Email Rule: Every Grade 12 must have a professional email address (e.g., sipho.ndlovu@gmail.com). Using a "nickname" email looks unprofessional to Universities.
Checking Status: Learners must log into the CAO website every week after October. If the status says "Waiting for Decision," you must be patient. If it says "Regret," you must immediately apply for your "Plan B" option.
