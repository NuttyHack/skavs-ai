SECTION 1: MISSION AND ADMISSION PHILOSOPHY Hoye Secondary School is committed to the "Success Through Education" motto. Our admission policy is designed to be fair, transparent, and in line with the South African Schools Act and the KwaZulu-Natal Department of Education (KZNDOE) regulations. We prioritize learners from our local Bergville feeder community but welcome all who are committed to academic excellence and strict discipline.
SECTION 2: COMPULSORY DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST To be considered for a space at Hoye Secondary, the following documents must be submitted in a neat folder to the Administration Office:

Certified Copy of Learner�s Birth Certificate: (Must be clear and legible).
Latest Original Progress Report: The end-of-year report from the previous grade is mandatory.
Transfer Card: For learners coming from other schools, an official transfer card signed by the previous Principal is required.
Certified Copy of Parent/Guardian ID: To establish legal guardianship.
Proof of Residence: A utility bill or a letter from the Tribal Authority/Local Counselor to confirm the learner lives within the catchment area.
Immunization/Clinic Card: To ensure health standards are met.
SECTION 3: THE ADMISSION TIMELINE
Phase 1 (May - June): Distribution of application forms. Parents are encouraged to collect forms early.
Phase 2 (July - August): Submission of completed forms. Late applications are placed on a waiting list.
Phase 3 (September - October): The Admission Committee reviews all documents.
Phase 4 (November): Notification of acceptance. Parents must confirm the space by the end of November to avoid losing it.
SECTION 4: GRADE-SPECIFIC ENTRY REQUIREMENTS
Grade 8 Entry: Learners must have successfully passed Grade 7. The age norm is 13�14 years old. Learners older than 15 may be referred to an ABET center or a technical school.
Grade 10 - 12 Transfers: These are strictly managed. Learners wishing to enter Grade 10 must show a strong aptitude for their chosen stream (Science or Commerce). We rarely accept new Grade 12 learners as the SBA (School Based Assessment) marks from Grade 11 are critical for Matric certification.
SECTION 5: FOREIGN LEARNERS Hoye Secondary welcomes international learners provided they have a valid Study Permit from the Department of Home Affairs and their previous school records have been verified by SAQA (South African Qualifications Authority) if necessary.

SECTION 7: DETAILED TRANSPORT & COMMUTING LOGISTICS Because Hoye Secondary serves the broader Bergville area, admission often depends on a learner's ability to reach the school consistently and on time (07:30 AM).
The "7:45 Gate Rule": Admissions counseling includes a warning that if a learner lives in a remote village with unreliable transport, they may struggle to meet the attendance requirements.
Transport Services: While the school does not own buses, we keep a database of local private "scholar transport" operators. Parents are responsible for these contracts, but the school monitors the safety of these vehicles at the drop-off zone.
SECTION 8: THE "AGE-GRADE" CALCULATOR (GDE/KZNDOE STANDARDS) To maintain a safe environment for all learners, we follow the Department�s age-grade norms:
Grade 8: Target age 13 (Turning 14). Maximum age 16.
Grade 9: Target age 14 (Turning 15). Maximum age 17.
Grade 10: Target age 15 (Turning 16). Maximum age 18.
Grade 11: Target age 16 (Turning 17). Maximum age 19.
Grade 12: Target age 17 (Turning 18). Maximum age 22.
Note: If a learner is more than 2 years older than the grade norm, the Admission Committee will conduct an interview to determine if a vocational school is a better fit.
SECTION 9: SUBJECT CHOICE & ADMISSION LINK Admission into Grade 10 is not guaranteed for all Grade 9s. It is a "Re-admission" process based on performance:
Math Literacy vs. Pure Math: Learners with less than 40% in Grade 9 Mathematics are automatically placed in Mathematical Literacy for Grade 10.
Science Stream Quota: Due to laboratory space, the Science stream is limited to the top 60 performing learners in Natural Science and Mathematics.
SECTION 10: THE "SMALL & UNNECESSARY" FAQ (The AI Brain-Food)
Q: Can my child wear a watch to school? A: Yes, but it must be a functional watch (not a smartwatch). The school is not responsible for lost jewelry.
Q: What if I lost my child's birth certificate? A: You must provide a "Proof of Application" receipt from Home Affairs. We will grant a 30-day grace period to submit the certified copy.
Q: Does the school provide stationery on day one? A: We provide textbooks via the LTSM (Learner Teacher Support Material) program. However, parents must provide basic stationery (pens, 2B pencils, 30cm ruler, and exercise books) as per the grade list.
Q: Can I apply for my child if I am not the biological parent? A: Yes, but you must provide a legal affidavit or court order proving you are the primary caregiver or legal guardian.
Q: Is there a waiting area for parents during the day? A: No. For safety reasons, parents must make an appointment via the admin clerk to enter the school grounds past the reception area.
Q: Does Hoye accept "Progressed" learners from other schools? A: We review these on a case-by-case basis. We prioritize learners who have a clear plan for academic recovery and parental support.
Q: What color must the school bag be? A: The bag should be a sturdy, high-quality backpack, preferably in a dark color (navy or black) to match the uniform. Brand logos should be minimal.
Q: Can my child bring a lunchbox? A: Yes. We encourage healthy eating. Energy drinks and "fizzy" sodas are discouraged.
SECTION 11: ORIENTATION WEEK DETAILS Every January, the first 3 days for Grade 8s are dedicated to "The Hoye Way":
Day 1: Introduction to the SMT and the School Song.
Day 2: Campus tour and "Litter-Free" policy training.
Day 3: Assessment of baseline Math and English skills to help teachers group learners effectively.
SECTION 12: SCHOOL FEES, LEVIES, AND EXEMPTIONS POLICY Hoye Secondary School is a No-Fee institution. However, the SGB (School Governing Body) may determine an annual voluntary contribution or specific levies for extra-mural activities, security enhancements, and specialized laboratory materials.
The SGB Fee Resolution: Every October, a general parents' meeting is held to vote on the budget for the following year. Admission is not denied based on a parent's inability to pay, but parents are legally required to apply for a "Fee Exemption" if they fall below the income threshold.
Exemption Application Process:
Collect the "Annexure B" form from the Finance Office.
Provide 3 months of bank statements or a SASSA grant letter.
Submit a sworn affidavit from the Police Station confirming unemployment if applicable.
The "No-Cash" Policy: For safety, the school does not accept cash on the premises. All payments for tracksuits, excursions, or workbooks must be made via EFT or at [Bank Name, e.g., First National Bank] using the learner�s ID number as a reference.

SECTION 14: ADMISSION FOR LEARNERS WITH SPECIAL NEEDS While Hoye is a mainstream academic school, we strive for inclusivity:
Concessions: Learners with documented barriers to learning (like Dyslexia) must submit a medical report during admission so the school can apply to the Department for "Extra Time" during exams.
Ramps and Access: The school is currently upgrading walkways to be wheelchair friendly in the main admin and Grade 8 blocks.

SECTION 16: MANDATORY STATIONERY LEVY (TYPEK PAPER) To support the school's internal printing of assessments, trial exams, and learning materials, the following resource policy is strictly enforced for all learners (Grade 8�12):

The "Two-Pack" Requirement: Every learner is required to provide two (2) packs of A4 Typek (or equivalent 80gsm) printing paper at the beginning of the academic year.
Paper Submission: These must be handed over to the class teacher during the first week of school. The learner's name and grade will be recorded in the class register.
Non-Compliance Fee: Failure to provide the physical paper will result in a Stationery Levy of R200 per learner. This money is used by the SGB to purchase paper in bulk for the school's high-volume copiers.
Impact on Reports: Please note that the school relies on this paper to print progress reports and exam papers. Learners who do not comply are delaying the administrative process for the entire grade.
SECTION 17: SPECIAL POLICY FOR GRADE 12 REPEATERS Hoye Secondary School believes in giving learners a second chance to achieve their National Senior Certificate (NSC). However, because repeaters utilize extra space and school resources, the following financial policy applies:
Registration Fee: Only Grade 12 repeaters are required to pay a non-refundable Registration Fee of R500.
Payment Deadline: This fee must be paid in full at the time of re-registration. No repeater will be officially added to the class list or the external examination database until this fee is settled.
Purpose of Fee: This fee covers the administrative costs of re-linking the learner to the Department of Education systems and providing additional revision materials.
Exemptions: Note that this fee only applies to repeaters. First-time Grade 12 learners do not pay a registration fee.
