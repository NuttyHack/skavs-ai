SECTION 1: THE HOYE PLEDGE & GENERAL PRINCIPLES Every learner admitted to Hoye Secondary School is bound by this Code of Conduct. While this text covers the primary regulations, learners and parents are encouraged to consult the Official 50-Page Hoye Code of Conduct Book available at the Administration Office for full legal definitions.
SECTION 2: ATTENDANCE & PUNCTUALITY (THE 7:15 RULE)

The School Day: The school day begins strictly at 07:15 AM. All learners must be inside the school premises and moving toward their assembly points or classrooms by this time.
Late Arrival: The gates close at 07:45 AM. Any learner arriving after 07:15 is considered late and will be penalized according to the merit/demerit system.
The "3-Day" Rule: Attendance is mandatory. Failure to attend school for three (3) consecutive days without a valid medical certificate or prior written permission from the Principal will lead to an immediate disciplinary hearing and potential expulsion from the school roll.
Session Attendance: Early morning (06:30 AM) and after-school sessions are mandatory. Skipping these is treated as "Bunking" and carries the same weight as skipping a normal school day.
SECTION 3: THE OFFICIAL UNIFORM (BOYS) The Hoye boy is a symbol of neatness and discipline.
Shirt: Official school blue shirt, tucked in at all times.
Trousers: Standard grey school trousers. They must be long enough to cover the ankles. "Skinny-fit" or altered trousers are strictly prohibited.
Belt: A plain black belt must be worn. No designer buckles or colored belts.
Socks: Long grey school socks.
Tie: The official Hoye school tie, worn neatly with the top button closed.
Jersey/Blazer: Only the official school jersey or blue blazer is allowed. No "hoodies" or non-school jackets.
Shoes: Traditional black school shoes. No sneakers, "all-stars," or pointed fashion shoes.
Hair Policy: Only a Brush Cut or Chiskop (fully shaved) is allowed. Hair must not be "trimmed" with designs, lines, or fades. Long hair is not acceptable unless a valid religious or cultural reason is provided in writing by the parents and approved by the SMT.
SECTION 4: THE OFFICIAL UNIFORM (GIRLS) The Hoye girl must represent the school with modesty and pride.
Skirts: Long school skirts that must reach just below the knees. Shortening of skirts is a disciplinary offense.
Trousers: Girls are permitted to wear Girls' Grey School Trousers. These are different from boys' trousers; they must be a feminine cut and not "skinny" or tight.
Shirt & Tie: Official school blue shirt and school tie.
Socks: Long grey school socks that must be pulled up to the knees.
Shoes: Traditional black school shoes (e.g., "Baby Dolls" or lace-ups).
Hair & Jewelry: Hair must be neat. No colorful extensions, loud hair accessories, or excessive jewelry. Only small gold/silver studs are allowed in the ears.
SECTION 5: DISCIPLINE & EXPULSION (ZERO TOLERANCE) Hoye Secondary maintains a "Safe Zone" policy. The following actions are Grade 3 Offenses and will lead to an Expulsion Hearing:
Fighting: Any physical altercation (fighting) either on or off school premises while in school uniform will lead to immediate suspension and recommended expulsion.
Substance Abuse: Smoking cigarettes, vaping, or the possession/use of alcohol or illegal drugs on school premises is strictly forbidden.
Criminal Record: Learners with an active criminal record are not eligible for admission to Hoye Secondary, as we prioritize the safety of our current learner body.
Insubordination: Failure to listen to or follow the instructions of a teacher, prefect, or member of staff is a serious offense. The "Bell" is the final authority; failure to respond to the bell immediately will result in disciplinary action.
SECTION 6: THE "SMALL BUT NECESSARY" RULES (FAQ)
Q: Can I wear a scarf in winter? A: Only if it is plain navy blue or black. No "fancy" or bright-colored scarves.
Q: What happens if I am caught smoking in uniform at the taxi rank? A: You are an ambassador for Hoye. Any behavior in uniform that brings the school's name into disrepute�even at the taxi rank�will lead to expulsion.
Q: Can I bring a cellphone to school? A: [Detail: e.g., Cellphones are brought at the learner's own risk and MUST be switched off and kept in the bag. If it rings in class, it will be confiscated for 1 term.]
Q: What if my parents can't afford the uniform right now? A: The parent must write a letter to the Principal explaining the situation and providing a date by which the uniform will be purchased.
SECTION 7: THE MERIT AND DEMERIT SYSTEM Hoye Secondary operates on a point-based system to track behavior.
Merits (Rewards): Learners receive +5 to +20 points for 100% attendance per term, placing in the Academic Top 10, or representing the school in sports.
Demerits (Penalties): * -5 Points: Late for class, littering, or incorrect socks.
o -20 Points: Insubordination, bunking a period, or foul language.
o -50 Points: Serious misconduct (leads to an immediate Parent-Teacher Meeting).
Accumulation: Accumulating -100 points leads to a formal Disciplinary Hearing with the SGB.
SECTION 8: THE "BELL" AND TRANSITION POLICY The school bell is the ultimate authority at Hoye Secondary.
The First Bell (07:15): Signals the start of the day. Learners must be at the assembly point.
Change of Periods: When the bell rings between periods, learners have exactly 3 minutes to move to their next classroom. Running in the corridors is strictly forbidden.
The "Silent Assembly": Once the Principal or a Teacher stands on the podium at assembly, all talking must cease immediately. Failure to do so results in a collective detention for the offending grade.
SECTION 9: CLASSROOM DECORUM & ETIQUETTE A classroom is a place of sacred learning.
Greeting: When a teacher or a visitor enters the room, all learners must stand up and greet them in unison ("Good morning, Sir/Ma'am") and remain standing until told to sit.
Eating/Drinking: No food or drink (including chewing gum) is allowed inside the classrooms. Water bottles are allowed only during extreme heatwaves.
Cleaning: The last period of the day is "Room Check." Each class is responsible for picking up litter and straightening desks before they are dismissed.
SECTION 10: CELLPHONE & ELECTRONIC DEVICE POLICY While we are a "Digital-Ready" school, cellphones are a major distraction.
Storage: Phones must be switched off and kept at the bottom of the school bag from 07:15 to 14:30.
Confiscation: If a phone is seen or heard (even a vibration), it will be confiscated by the teacher.
Recovery: Confiscated phones will only be returned to the Parent/Legal Guardian after a period of 30 days and a fine of R100 (contributed to the school's sports fund).
Earphones: Wearing earphones or headphones in uniform is strictly prohibited and leads to immediate confiscation.
SECTION 11: OUT-OF-BOUNDS AREAS For the safety of all learners, the following areas are "Out-of-Bounds" during break and before/after school:
The Staffroom and Staff Toilets.
Behind the Science Laboratories.
The School Kitchen (unless you are a kitchen helper).
The parking lot (Learners may not sit in or hang around teachers' cars).
SECTION 12: SOCIAL MEDIA & THE "HOYE BRAND" Any learner wearing the Hoye Secondary uniform is an ambassador of the school.
TikTok/Facebook/WhatsApp: Posting videos of yourself dancing, "clowning," or using bad language while in school uniform is a Level 2 offense.
Groups: Any WhatsApp group created using the school's name must have at least one teacher or class representative as an admin to prevent cyber-bullying.
SECTION 13: THE LITTER-FREE CAMPUS POLICY Hoye pride starts with a clean campus.
Spot-Fines: Any learner caught throwing a wrapper or bottle on the ground will be required to spend their break time picking up litter across the entire sports field.
Vandalism: Writing on desks ("Graffiti") or scratching walls is a serious offense. Parents will be billed for the full cost of repainting or sanding the desk.
SECTION 14: THE PREFECT (RCL) AUTHORITY The Representative Council of Learners (RCL) are the chosen leaders of the school.
Respect: Disrespecting an RCL member is treated the same as disrespecting a teacher.
Reporting: RCL members do not have the power to "expel" or "hit" students, but they have a direct line to the Deputy Principal (Mr. S.T. Mleshi) to report misconduct.
SECTION 15: THE "PARENTAL COMPACT" & APPEALS
The Final Word: The Principal (Mrs. R.Z. Mvelase) has the final authority on all internal disciplinary matters.
Appeals: If a parent is unhappy with a disciplinary decision, they must submit a formal letter of appeal to the SGB Chairman within 48 hours.
Accurate Info: For matters not covered here, please refer to the Official 50-Page Code of Conduct Book available at the front desk.
