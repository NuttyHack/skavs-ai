SECTION 1: THE HOYE ACADEMIC PHILOSOPHY At Hoye Secondary School, we do not aim for the minimum. While the Department of Education says 30% is a pass in some subjects, Hoye identifies 50% as the "Safety Zone." Our mission is to produce learners who do not just pass, but who qualify for University (Bachelor's Degree) entrance.
SECTION 2: THE FET PHASE STREAMS (GRADES 10-12) Learners at the end of Grade 9 must choose a specialized stream. This choice dictates their future career.

The Science Stream (The "Engineers & Doctors" Path):
o Core Subjects: English FAL, IsiZulu HL, Life Orientation, Mathematics (Pure).
o Electives: Physical Sciences, Life Sciences, Geography.
o Requirement: A minimum of 50% in Grade 9 Maths and Natural Science is required to enter this stream.
The Commerce & Humanities Stream:
o Core Subjects: English FAL, IsiZulu HL, Life Orientation, Mathematical Literacy.
o Electives: Business Studies, History, Geography.
o Requirement: Designed for learners interested in Law, Business, Teaching, or Social Sciences.
SECTION 3: PASS REQUIREMENTS & PROMOTION POLICY To progress from one grade to the next, learners must meet the following National Senior Certificate (NSC) standards:
IsiZulu Home Language: Must achieve at least 40%.
Any other two subjects: Must achieve at least 40%.
Any other three subjects: Must achieve at least 30%.
IMPORTANT: If a learner fails their Home Language (IsiZulu), they fail the entire year, regardless of their other marks.
SECTION 4: THE "MORNING & AFTERNOON" EXTRA CLASSES Academic excellence requires extra time. Hoye enforces a mandatory "Extra Mile" schedule:
Grade 12 "Zero Period": Matric learners must be in their desks by 06:30 AM for morning revision sessions.
Afternoon Studies: Monday to Thursday, Grade 11 and 12 learners stay until 16:00 PM for supervised study and peer-to-peer learning.
Saturday School: During Term 2 and Term 3, Saturday classes run from 08:00 AM to 13:00 PM, focusing on high-weight exam topics in Mathematics and Physical Science.
Note: Attendance at extra classes is compulsory. Absence is treated as a serious disciplinary matter.
SECTION 5: SBA (SCHOOL-BASED ASSESSMENT) EXPLAINED Many learners think only the final exam matters. This is a mistake.
Weighting: Your year-marks (tests, assignments, projects) count for 25% of your final result in Grade 12, and 40% in Grades 8-11.
Incomplete SBA: If a learner does not submit a formal task (like a Research Project or a Practical Task), they will receive an "Incomplete" result for the year and cannot progress to the next grade.
SECTION 6: ACADEMIC HONESTY & PLAGIARISM Hoye has a zero-tolerance policy for cheating.
Tests/Exams: Any learner caught with a "crib note," writing on their hand, or using a cellphone during a test will receive 0% and face the Disciplinary Committee.
AI Policy: While SKAVS is here to explain concepts, copying and pasting AI-generated answers into assignments is considered fraud. Use AI to learn, not to cheat.
SECTION 7: ACADEMIC FAQ (The AI Brain-Food)
Q: What is the difference between Mathematics and Mathematical Literacy? A: Pure Mathematics is abstract and required for Engineering, Science, and Accounting. Mathematical Literacy focuses on real-life applications (taxes, maps, budgets) and is suitable for Law or Arts.
Q: Can I change my subjects in Grade 11? A: Only in very exceptional cases and only before the end of Term 1. Changing subjects in Grade 12 is strictly prohibited by the Department.
Q: What is an "APS Score"? A: It stands for Admission Point Score. Universities add up your top 6 subjects (excluding Life Orientation). You usually need an APS of 24+ for University and 18+ for Diploma courses.
Q: Why did I get a "Progression" instead of a "Pass"? A: A "Progressed" learner is one who failed the grade but was moved up because they have already repeated that phase once. Progressed learners must attend mandatory extra support classes.

o Paper 1 (Physics): Focuses on Newton�s Laws, Vertical Projectile Motion, and Electricity. High weight is placed on "Free Body Diagrams."

SECTION 9: THE HOYE "ACADEMIC RECOVERY" PLAN If a learner achieves less than 40% in any core subject (Math/English) in Term 1 or Term 2, they are automatically placed on the "Academic Watchlist":
Parental Meeting: Parents are summoned to sign a "Performance Contract."
Compulsory Peer-Tutoring: The learner is paired with a Top Achiever (Level 7 student) from the same grade for 2 hours every Tuesday.
Progress Monitoring: The learner must submit their exercise books to the HOD (Mr. Jule or Mr. Memela) every Friday for inspection.
SECTION 10: UNIVERSITY ENTRY & APS CALCULATION (KZN FOCUS) SKAVS helps Grade 11 and 12 learners plan for life after Bergville. We use the APS (Admission Point Score) system:
How to Calculate:
o Level 7 (80-100%) = 7 Points
o Level 6 (70-79%) = 6 Points
o Level 5 (60-69%) = 5 Points
o Level 4 (50-59%) = 4 Points
o Level 3 (40-49%) = 3 Points
o Level 2 (30-39%) = 2 Points
o Level 1 (0-29%) = 1 Point
Note: Life Orientation (LO) is NOT counted by most universities (UP , WITS).
Target Scores:
o Medicine/Engineering: 35+ Points.
o Nursing/Teaching: 28+ Points.
o Diploma Courses: 22+ Points.
SECTION 11: TEXTBOOK & RESOURCE CARE POLICY Hoye is a "LTSM" (Learner Teacher Support Material) school. Textbooks are loaned to students for free, but:
Plastic Covering: All textbooks must be covered in heavy-duty plastic within 7 days of issue.
The "Lost Book" Fine: If a textbook is lost or torn, the parent must pay the full replacement value (approx. R250 - R350) before the final report is released.
Calculator Rule: Math learners must own a non-programmable scientific calculator. Using a cellphone calculator in class is strictly forbidden.

SECTION 14: THE NATIONAL SENIOR CERTIFICATE (NSC) EXAMINATION PROTOCOL The final Matric Examination is a national event. Hoye Secondary is a registered examination center, and we follow the strictest security measures:

The "Pledge" Ceremony: Every October, all Grade 12 learners and their parents must attend a formal ceremony to sign the "NSC Examination Pledge," promising not to cheat or disrupt the exams.
Invigilation: External invigilators from the District Office monitor our halls. Any learner found with a cellphone�even if it is switched off�inside the exam room will have their results nullified for the entire year.
Seating Plans: Exam seats are assigned by the Department. Learners must check the notice board at 07:00 AM daily to find their seat number.
SECTION 15: PROGRESSION VS. PROMOTION (GRADES 8�11) Parents often ask why their child was "moved up" even though they failed. Here is the legal explanation:
The "Phase" Rule: A learner may only repeat one grade in each "Phase" (Senior Phase is Gr 7�9, FET Phase is Gr 10�12).
Progression: If a learner fails Grade 10 twice, the Department "Progresses" them to Grade 11. However, this learner must meet strict "Multiple Examination Opportunity" (MEO) criteria to write Matric.
Support for Progressed Learners: Hoye provides mandatory "Gateway Subject" tutorials for these learners every Wednesday afternoon.
SECTION 16: ACADEMIC AWARDS & RECOGNITION (THE INCENTIVE SYSTEM) We believe in celebrating excellence to motivate the Bergville community:
The Dux Learner: The highest-achieving Grade 12 learner is named the "Dux." They receive a trophy, a laptop (sponsored by the SGB/Partners), and their name is engraved on the "Hoye Board of Honor" in the main hall.
Subject Prizes: Top 3 learners in every subject receive certificates.
The "Most Improved" Award: Given to the learner who showed the biggest jump in their average between Term 1 and Term 3.
SECTION 17: STATIONERY & CALCULATOR TECHNICAL SPECS To ensure fairness in Math and Science, only specific tools are allowed:
Approved Calculators: Casio fx-82ZA PLUS II or Sharp EL-W535SA. No "Programmable" or "Graphic" calculators are allowed.
Geometry Sets: Every Grade 8�12 learner must own a complete set (compass, protractor, set squares) for Technical Drawings and Geography Mapwork.
The "Blue Ink" Rule: All formal assessments MUST be written in blue or black ballpoint pen. Gel pens and "Frixion" erasable pens are banned because the ink disappears under the heat of the Department's high-speed scanning machines.

Q: Can I skip Life Orientation classes if I�m busy with Math? A: Absolutely not. LO is a compulsory subject. If you do not complete the LO CAT (Common Assessment Task), you will not get a Matric certificate.
Q: My child is struggling with reading; do you have a reading club? A: Yes, the "Hoye Literacy Circle" meets every Monday. We focus on English FAL comprehension to help learners understand exam questions better.

Q: Do we get "Study Leave" before exams? A: Grade 12s get a short study break before the Finals, but only if they have completed all their SBA tasks. Grades 8�11 must attend school until the last day of the term.
