SECTION 1: THE HOYE SPIRIT & IDENTITY Hoye Secondary School is more than just a place of books; it is a guardian of our heritage in the heart of Bergville. Our identity is built on the motto "Success Through Education," but our soul is found in our traditions, our respect for our elders, and our powerful voices.
SECTION 2: THE REALITY OF SPORTS AT HOYE At Hoye Secondary, we believe in transparency with our parents and the community.

Current Status: Due to a lack of specialized equipment and a current lack of corporate sponsorship, the school does not offer formal sporting codes (such as league soccer, netball, or rugby) at this time.
The Vision for Sport: The SGB and the Physical Education (LO) teachers are actively seeking local Bergville business partners to sponsor kits, balls, and the leveling of our grounds.
Informal Recreation: While we lack professional equipment, learners are encouraged to stay active during break times in designated safe play areas.
SECTION 3: THE CULTURAL POWERHOUSE (INDLAMU) Where we lack in sports, we lead in Culture. Hoye is famous in the district for our traditional Zulu dancing (Indlamu).
The Team: Our Indlamu group is a disciplined unit of warriors and dancers who represent the school at local and district competitions.
The Training: Practices take place after school, focusing on the precision of the high kick, the rhythm of the drum, and the pride of our ancestors.
Competitions: We participate in annual cultural festivals in the Uthukela District, often bringing home trophies for our authentic performance and traditional attire.
SECTION 4: CHORAL EXCELLENCE & GOSPEL CHOIR The voices of Hoye learners are our greatest instrument.
The Gospel Choir: Our learners have a deep spiritual connection to Gospel music. The school choir performs at all major school events, including the Morning Assembly and the Year-End Prize Giving.
Competitions: We enter regional Choral and Gospel competitions. Our choir is known for its "Hoye Harmony"�a unique sound that blends traditional Zulu melodies with modern Gospel arrangements.
SECTION 5: WEDDING & TRADITIONAL CELEBRATIONS Hoye Secondary is a school that celebrates life. One of our most unique cultural activities is our participation in Traditional Wedding Competitions (Ukucula kwemishado).
Community Connection: We teach our learners the songs and dances traditionally performed at weddings in the Bergville area. This ensures that the "Hoye Graduate" is a well-rounded member of the community who knows how to behave and lead at family ceremonies.
The Skill: This isn't just "fun"; it teaches our learners public speaking, coordination, and the preservation of Zulu customs (Amagugu esizwe).

SECTION 7: HERITAGE DAY (USUKU LWEGUGU) September is the most vibrant month at Hoye.
The Celebration: On Heritage Day, the "No-Uniform" rule is applied, and every learner and teacher is encouraged to wear their full traditional Zulu attire (Amabeshu, Izinqindi, and Ubuhlalu).
The Feast: We celebrate with traditional food and a massive "Dance-Off" where the different grades compete in Indlamu and traditional singing.
SECTION 8: PRIZE GIVING & COMMUNITY DAY Every year, we invite the parents of Bergville to see the fruits of our labor.
Awards: We don't just reward the "Top 1" in Math; we reward the "Most Disciplined," the "Best Cultural Performer," and the "Cleanest Learner."
Guest of Honor: We often invite local Chiefs (Amakhosi) or successful business owners to inspire the children.
SECTION 9: COMMUNITY SERVICE & SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY Hoye learners are taught to give back.
Litter Campaigns: We occasionally organize "Clean-Up Bergville" walks to ensure our local environment is respected.
Support for the Needy: During the winter months, the school often organizes "Coat Drives" or food collections for the most vulnerable families in our learner body.
SECTION 10: CULTURE & COMMUNITY FAQ
Q: Can I join the Indlamu team if I am in Grade 8? A: Yes! We start training learners early so they can be leaders by the time they reach Grade 12.
Q: Does the school have a bus for cultural trips? A: We currently hire local transport for competitions. Part of our fundraising goes toward these trips.
Q: Why don't we have soccer? A: As mentioned, we lack the necessary sponsorship for kits and field maintenance. We are open to any parents who want to help start a "Volunteer Coaching" program!
Q: Is the Gospel Choir only for Christians? A: No. Any learner who has a voice and a passion for the music is welcome to join.
